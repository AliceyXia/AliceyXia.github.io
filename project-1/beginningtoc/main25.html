The painter Cy Twombly quotes John Crowe Ransom, on a scrap of paper:
“The image cannot be disposed of a primordial freshness which ideas can never claim.”

画家, 赛-汤伯利 (Cy Twombly) 曾在一张废纸上引用了约翰-克劳-兰瑟姆(John Crowe Ransom) 的话。
"图像无法处置一种原始的新鲜感，这是思想永远无法要求的"。



Easy and appropriate thing for a painter to say.
Cy Twombly uses text in some of his drawings and paintings, usually poetry, usually Dante.
Many men and women have written long essays and lectures on the ideas they see expressed in Twombly’s work

对于一个画家来说，这句话通常很容易也很合适。赛-汤伯利在他的一些绘画作品中使用了文字，通常是诗歌，通常是但丁。
许多男人和女人都写了长长的文章和讲座，论述他们在汤伯利的作品中看到的思想表达。



Bachelard’s sentence simply says this: origins (beginnings) have consequences (endings).

巴赫拉(Bachelard) 的句子简单地解释了：有起源（始）有后果（终）。



The poem is the consequence of its origins.
Give me the fruit and I will take from it a seed and plant it and watch grow the tree from which it fell.

这首诗是其起源的结果。
把果实给我，我将从它身上取下一粒种子，把它种下去，然后看着那落下种子成长为一颗树。
